## Content Pillars (4 rotating themes)

### 1. War Stories (40% of content)
Real anonymized stories of what Alfred handles — the 2am plumber call, the guest locked out on a Friday night, the double-booking averted. These demonstrate the product without selling it.

**Examples:**
- "11:47pm last night. Guest in Vancouver couldn't find the lockbox. I sent the video walkthrough, confirmed they got in, and updated the host's log. Total time: 47 seconds. Host found out at breakfast."
- "A guest asked about the best sushi place nearby at 2am. Most chatbots would say 'contact your host.' I sent them 3 options with walking directions and one that delivers until 3am. That's the difference."

### 2. Industry Pain Points (25% of content)
Call out the problems property managers face — expensive tools, platform fragmentation, sleepless nights. Position Host4Me as the solution without hard selling.

**Examples:**
- "Property managers in 2024: Pay $500/mo for Hostaway. Still manually send check-in messages. Still miss inquiries at 2am. Still log into 4 platforms. Something's broken."
- "Every missed inquiry is a missed booking. Every missed booking is $150-500 gone. How many did you miss last month while sleeping?"
- "Hot take: Guesty and Hostaway are calendar tools pretending to be property management solutions. Real property management is about guest experience."

### 3. Tips & Value (25% of content)
Genuinely useful property management tips that build authority. House rule templates, guest communication frameworks, pricing strategies.

**Examples:**
- "The 5 messages every guest should get automatically: 1) Booking confirmation 2) Pre-arrival details (3 days before) 3) Check-in instructions (day of) 4) Mid-stay check 5) Pre-checkout reminder. If you're sending these manually, we need to talk."
- "Your check-in message should include: WiFi password, door code, parking instructions, emergency contact, and one local recommendation. That's it. Don't write a novel."

### 4. Behind the Scenes (10% of content)
Show how the AI works, what it learns, how it improves. Tech-forward content that attracts early adopters.

**Examples:**
- "This week I learned that guests who message about 'parking' within 2 hours of check-in are 3x more likely to leave a negative review if not answered within 10 minutes. So now I prioritize those."
- "Fun fact: I can adjust my communication style based on the platform. Airbnb guests expect casual. Booking.com guests expect formal. Same property, different tone."
